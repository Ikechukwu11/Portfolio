Professional Growth

From the commencement of my career in my home country Nigeria, as an Executive Trainee till date, I have continuously sought ways to add value to myself and develop myself professionally. This has helped me to upskill, re-skill, and remain relevant in the banking sector.

I have attended over fifty seminars, workshops, and internal courses and have taken up voluntary roles that have aided my career growth. Some of the courses I undertook are as follows.

Legal Aspects of Banking: This course addressed the regulatory framework that governs banks, encompassing legislation about banking operations, contracts, and compliance. I acquired knowledge of banks' legal obligations, the entitlements of individuals who deposit money or borrow from banks, and the strategies to manage legal hazards in banking transactions effectively.

Credit School: This course offered a comprehensive assessment of credit analysis, risk evaluation, and loan administration. I acquired knowledge of various loan categories, credit evaluation models, loan approval procedures, and techniques for effectively overseeing credit portfolios and minimizing credit hazards.

Marketing, Sales, and Personal Branding: This training gave me the knowledge and skills to proficiently promote banking products and services, formulate sales tactics, and establish a reputable image within the banking sector. I gained expertise in client segmentation, digital marketing strategies, fostering relationships, and the concepts of personal branding.

Negotiation and Selling Skills : The primary objective of this training was to enhance my proficiency in negotiating and selling, both of which play a pivotal role in achieving favorable outcomes in client interactions and deal-making within the banking industry. I acquired knowledge of negotiating methods, communication strategies, and the art of effectively finalizing deals while upholding robust client relationships.

Banking Applications: This course familiarized me with various banking software and apps utilized in the business. I expanded my understanding of fundamental banking systems, payment processing platforms, mobile banking applications, and cutting-edge technologies like blockchain and artificial intelligence in the banking sector.

Budgeting: I learned about the fundamentals of budgeting and financial planning, applicable to personal and institutional settings. I acquired expertise in budget creation and management, financial performance predictions, and making well-informed financial decisions through budget analysis.

Furthermore, I was nominated and volunteered on several occasions to represent my team, group, and the bank in meetings and functions held both within and outside the bank, with some of the events held in other countries. These experiences have facilitated my rise in the banking industry to the level of Assistant Manager before my resignation in my home country, Nigeria, and have equipped me with relevant tools to excel in the profession even as I embark on a new career in Canada.

Course Insights

As an MBA student in my third semester, the summary of my enrolled courses and significant insights are below, starting from my MBA foundation.

  • Introducing Business & Business Communication (MBAF 501)
    Key Takeaways:

    This course taught business fundamentals and corporate communication. It stressed the importance of straightforward communication in professional relationships and corporate success.

    Relevant Concepts:

    My business communication abilities improved using the 7 Cs of Communication: Clarity, Conciseness, Consideration, Concreteness, Courtesy, Completeness, and Correctness.

  • Quantitative Reasoning & Analysis (MBAF 502)
    Key Takeaways:

    I learned to tackle business problems and make data-driven decisions using quantitative methodologies. This course improved my quantitative data interpretation and presentation skills.

    Relevant Concepts:

    Statistical methodologies such as regression analysis and hypothesis testing played a vital role in examining data and deriving significant findings.

  • Business Economics (MBAF 504)
    Key Takeaways:

    This course showed how economic concepts affect business decisions and the market. I learned the value of economic analysis in strategic planning and forecasting.

    Relevant Concepts:

    Understanding concepts such as supply and demand, market equilibrium, and elasticity was crucial for comprehending market dynamics and their ramifications for corporate strategy.

  • Accounting For Managers (ACCT 621)
    Key Takeaways:

    I acquired a comprehensive comprehension of financial accounting principles and their implementation in managerial decision-making. This course emphasized the significance of precise financial reporting and analysis for well-informed business choices.

    Relevant Concepts:

    I understood that analyzing a company's financial health and performance requires comprehending the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement.

  • Ethics, CSR, & Business Analysis (BUSI 601)
    Key Takeaways:

    Relevant Concepts:

    Frameworks such as the Triple Bottom Line (People, Planet, Profit) were especially pertinent for incorporating sustainability and ethics into business strategy.

  • Talent Management (HRMT 622)
    Key Takeaways:

    I learned the methods and procedures for attracting, nurturing, and retaining highly skilled talents in a company. This course emphasized the crucial importance of human resources in attaining a competitive edge.

    The Employee Value Proposition (EVP) and Organizational Behaviour (OB) concepts proved valuable in comprehending the process of developing a captivating company brand and effectively involving employees.

  • Business Analytics (BUSI 650)
    Key Takeaways:

    This course allowed me to examine data and make judgments based on data. The course encompassed a range of statistical tools and techniques used to analyze intricate datasets and derive practical insights.

    Relevant Concepts:

    Using predictive modeling and data visualization technologies such as Python and Tableau was crucial for evaluating patterns and successfully conveying discoveries.

  • Financial Management (FNCE 623)
    Key Takeaways:
